1. abstain — avoid doing something

Many voters chose to abstain from the election due to lack of trust.

2. approbation — approval; praise

The scientist’s breakthrough received widespread approbation.

3. cherish — deeply value or protect

He cherished the old letters his grandmother had written to him.

4. corroborate — confirm; support with evidence

Multiple witnesses corroborated her version of the events.

5. disparate — fundamentally different

The two teams had disparate approaches to the project.

6. emulate — imitate to match or surpass

She tried to emulate her mentor’s calm leadership style.

7. enervate — weaken; drain energy

The extreme heat enervated the athletes during the marathon.

8. ephemeral — short-lived

Fame on social media is often ephemeral.

9. fervid — extremely passionate

His fervid speech rallied the entire crowd.

10. garrulous — excessively talkative

The garrulous host dominated every conversation.

11. incendiary — causing conflict; provocative

His incendiary remarks sparked a heated debate.

12. inimical — harmful; hostile

A hostile work environment is inimical to productivity.

13. intimate — suggest subtly

She intimated that she might be looking for a new job.

14. invigorate — energize; give strength

A cold shower in the morning invigorates him.

15. mitigate — reduce severity

New safety measures were implemented to mitigate risks.

16. obsolete — outdated; no longer useful

DVD players have become largely obsolete.

17. opaque — unclear; difficult to understand

The professor’s explanation was so opaque that no one understood it.

18. paradigmatic — serving as a model

Her research became paradigmatic for future studies in the field.

19. pedantic — overly focused on minor details

His pedantic corrections annoyed everyone in the group.

20. placid — calm; peaceful

The lake was placid, reflecting the mountains perfectly.

21. polemical — strongly argumentative or controversial

He wrote a polemical article criticizing government policies.

22. precipitate — cause suddenly

His careless comments precipitated a major argument.

23. profundity — deep insight or wisdom

The poem’s profundity impressed the entire class.

24. prophetic — accurately predicting the future

Her warning proved prophetic when the market collapsed weeks later.

25. prudent — careful; wise in decisions

It’s prudent to save money for emergencies.

26. punctilious — extremely attentive to detail

The editor was punctilious about grammar and punctuation.

27. recondite — difficult to understand; obscure

The philosopher’s recondite theories puzzled his students.

28. scrupulous — careful; morally principled

She was scrupulous about reporting accurate data.

29. tranquil — peaceful; calm

He enjoyed the tranquil atmosphere of the remote cabin.

30. vacillate — hesitate; waver between options

She vacillated for days before accepting the offer.
